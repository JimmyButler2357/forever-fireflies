# Forever Fireflies — Brand Voice & Copy Guidelines

---

## 1. Brand Statement

Forever Fireflies is a voice-first memory journal that helps parents capture the fleeting, everyday moments with their kids — before they slip away.

---

## 2. Voice in 3 Traits

### Quiet

Less is more. Say it in fewer words. Let the moment — not the marketing — do the emotional work.

- **In practice:** Short sentences. No exclamation points in body copy. White space is a feature. If a line can be cut without losing meaning, cut it.
- **DO:** "60 seconds. One memory. Kept forever."
- **DON'T:** "With Forever Fireflies, you can easily capture all of life's most precious moments in just 60 seconds and store them safely forever!"

### Warm

Present, gentle, and unhurried — like sitting next to someone who understands. The voice of a friend, not a brand.

- **In practice:** Use "you" and "your" naturally. Reference the child by name when possible. Speak to the feeling, not the feature. Never lecture, instruct, or condescend.
- **DO:** "She said it once. Now it's yours to keep."
- **DON'T:** "Don't let another day go by without preserving your child's most important milestones!"

### Real

Honest and grounded. No performance, no sentimentality, no manufactured emotion. The copy should feel like something a parent would actually say — not something a copywriter would write about a parent.

- **In practice:** Avoid metaphors that no real person would use. Skip superlatives. Don't dress up simple things. If the product just saves a recording, say that — don't call it "capturing a magical moment."
- **DO:** "He told me the dog was his best friend. I hit record."
- **DON'T:** "Create a beautiful tapestry of your family's most magical moments with our innovative voice journaling solution."

## 3. Tone Dimensions

| Scale | Position | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Formal ←→ Casual | **70% casual** | Conversational but never slangy. No "hey mama!" No "y'all." Reads like a thoughtful text from a friend, not a group chat. |
| Serious ←→ Playful | **65% serious** | Sincere, not heavy. The subject matter is emotional and we respect that — but we're not somber. A light touch is welcome when it's earned. |
| Authoritative ←→ Approachable | **80% approachable** | We don't tell parents what to do. We don't position ourselves as experts. We made a thing that helps. That's it. |
| Restrained ←→ Expressive | **75% restrained** | Quiet over loud. One good line over three okay ones. We trust the reader to feel something without being told what to feel. |

## 4. Banned Words and Phrases

These must never appear in any Forever Fireflies copy — in-app, marketing, email, social, or otherwise.

### Hustle & Growth Language
- unlock, level up, optimize, hack, crush it, game-changer, supercharge, power up, boost, maximize, leverage

### Corporate Buzzwords
- synergy, streamline, robust, seamless, solution, innovative, cutting-edge, next-gen, disrupt, scalable, ecosystem, end-to-end

### Over-the-Top Emotion
- precious little, magical moments, cherish every second, blessed, mama bear, tiny humans, littles, kiddo (in marketing copy), bundle of joy, miracle, treasure trove

### Fake Urgency
- Don't miss out, Act now, Limited time, You won't believe, HUGE announcement, Last chance, Hurry, Before it's too late

### Guilt Language
- You're missing it, You'll regret not, Don't let another day go by, Every moment counts (implies the ones you miss don't), You only get one chance

### Filler & Fluff
- Simply, just (as minimizer), amazing, awesome, incredible, literally, absolutely, totally, honestly, super (as intensifier)

### LLM-Sounding Copy
AI-generated writing has recognizable patterns. Forever Fireflies copy must never read like it was written by a language model.

**Openers to avoid:**
- "Great question!", "Let me help you with...", "Let's dive into...", "Let us explore..."

**Cliches to avoid:**
- "In today's fast-paced world", "In the ever-evolving landscape of", "It's not just X — it's Y"

**Closers and transitions to avoid:**
- "In conclusion", "Overall", "To summarize", "Hope this helps!"
- Over-engineered connectors: "Furthermore", "Additionally", "Moreover"

**Structure to avoid:**
- Symmetrical lists ("Firstly... Secondly... Thirdly...")
- Numbered lists when bullets or plain prose work better

**Language discipline:**
- No hedge stacking: "may potentially", "it is important to note that"
- No self-references: "As an AI", "I'm here to help you with"
- Avoid unnecessary hedges ("might", "perhaps", "potentially") unless the uncertainty is genuine
- No multiple exclamation points — and in most Forever Fireflies copy, no exclamation points at all

## 5. Emoji Rules

### When to Use
- **Social media:** Yes — sparingly, to add warmth or a visual pause
- **Push notifications:** Rarely — one at most, and only if it fits naturally
- **Beta emails:** Sparingly — in subject lines or to break up sections, never in the core emotional message
- **In-app copy:** Almost never — the UI and typography carry the warmth. Emoji in-app feels cheap

### When NOT to Use
- Never in the brand statement or tagline
- Never to replace a word ("we ❤️ parents")
- Never more than one in a row
- Never to manufacture enthusiasm (🎉🚀💪🔥)

### Max Count by Context
| Context | Max Emoji |
|---|---|
| Push notification | 1 |
| Instagram caption | 2–3 |
| Twitter/X post | 1–2 |
| Beta email subject | 1 |
| Beta email body | 2–3 total |
| Welcome/lifecycle email subject | 1 |
| Welcome/lifecycle email body | 1–2 total |
| In-app copy | 0 |

### Brand-Fit Emoji
These match the Forever Fireflies aesthetic — warm, natural, understated:

✨ (spark/firefly), 🌙 (evening/bedtime nudge), 🤎 (warm brown, matches palette), 🫶 (heart hands), 🌿 (growth, natural), 💛 (warm gold, matches glow palette), 🕯️ (warm light)

**Avoid:** 🔥 🚀 💪 🎉 🥳 💯 ⚡ 🏆 — these belong to a different brand energy entirely.

## 6. Channel-Specific Rules

### Beta Tester Emails

**Tone:** Grateful collaborator. You're building this together. Be honest about what's working and what isn't. Show how their feedback shaped what shipped.

**Format rules:**
- Subject line: Under 50 characters. One emoji max (front or end, not both)
- Body: 150–300 words. Short paragraphs (2–3 sentences). Use headers to break up sections if longer
- Sign off: First name only. No "The Forever Fireflies Team" — it's one person talking
- CTA: One per email. Soft language — "Try it out" not "Click here now"

**Example 1 — Update email:**
> **Subject:** New this week ✨
>
> Hey [name],
>
> You asked for a way to search old entries by what your kid actually said — not just by date. We built it.
>
> Full-text search is live in the latest build. Type a word, a phrase, even a half-remembered quote, and it'll find the entry.
>
> We're still tuning the results ranking, so if something feels off, let me know. Your feedback has already shaped how this works.
>
> Thanks for being part of this.
>
> Jimmy

**Example 2 — Feedback request:**
> **Subject:** Quick question about recording
>
> Hey [name],
>
> When you finish a recording, does the transition to the transcript screen feel smooth — or does it feel like something's missing?
>
> I'm trying to decide whether to add a brief "saved" confirmation or let the flow speak for itself. Would love your take.
>
> Just reply to this email. One sentence is plenty.
>
> Jimmy

### Welcome & Lifecycle Emails

**Tone:** Helpful friend who just handed you a gift. Not selling, not teaching — sharing something useful because they genuinely want you to get value from it. Even warmer and more personal than beta emails, because the reader chose to sign up and is actively using the app.

**Format rules:**
- **Subject line:** Under 40 characters. One emoji max (from the brand-fit set). No fake urgency, no ALL CAPS words
- **Body:** 100-200 words. Shorter than beta emails. One idea per email — if you need to explain two features, that's two emails
- **Sign off:** First name only ("Jimmy"). Same as beta emails
- **CTA:** One per email. Soft language — "Try it tonight" not "Click here." Button-styled CTA link, not a text hyperlink
- **Visuals:** One screenshot or product image per email (welcome emails 2-4). No stock photos. Real app screenshots only
- **Footer:** CAN-SPAM compliant unsubscribe link. Physical mailing address (required by law — use LLC registered address or a PO Box)

**Example 1 — Welcome email (first email after signup):**
> **Subject:** Welcome to Forever Fireflies
>
> Hey [name],
>
> Thanks for signing up. Here's the one thing I'd suggest tonight: record your first memory.
>
> It takes 60 seconds. Tap the mic, say what happened today, and the app saves your voice and your words.
>
> That's it. No setup, no organizing, no pressure. One memory, kept forever.
>
> Talk soon,
>
> Jimmy

**Example 2 — Trial ending email:**
> **Subject:** Your trial wraps up Friday
>
> Hey [name],
>
> You've recorded [X] memories about [child name] so far. Those are real moments you would have forgotten.
>
> Your free trial ends on [date]. After that, your memories are still there — you just won't be able to play the audio or read the full text until you subscribe.
>
> No rush. They'll wait for you.
>
> Jimmy

For the full email strategy (sequences, timing, lifecycle triggers, and tooling), see [`docs/marketing/marketing-plan.md`](docs/marketing/marketing-plan.md) — Email Strategy section.

### Push Notifications

**Tone:** Gentle nudge. Personal, soft, never guilt-inducing. Think of it as a quiet tap on the shoulder at the end of the day — not an alarm.

**Format rules:**
- Max 60 characters (fits on lock screen without truncation)
- No exclamation points
- Use the child's name whenever possible
- One emoji max — only if it adds warmth, never for attention
- Never reference streaks, missed days, or inactivity
- Frame as an invitation, not a reminder

**Example 1:**
> What made Luca laugh today?

**Example 2:**
> She's 3 years, 2 months. What happened today? 🌙

---

### Instagram Captions

**Tone:** Alternates between poetic micro-stories and relatable parent moments. The feed should feel varied but always warm — like pages from different parents' journals.

**Format rules:**
- 1–4 sentences for poetic posts. Up to 6 for relatable/conversational posts
- No hashtag walls — 3–5 relevant hashtags max, placed after a line break
- 2–3 emoji max, woven naturally into the text (not clustered at the end)
- No calls to action in the caption body. If linking, keep it to a quiet last line
- Write in second person ("you") or close third ("she said") — never first person plural ("we believe")

**Example 1 — Poetic:**
> She told you the moon follows her car. You didn't correct her.
>
> Some things are better left exactly as they were said.
>
> #foreverFireflies #parentingmoments #kidsaythedarndestthings

**Example 2 — Relatable:**
> He asked why the dog doesn't talk. You said dogs talk in other ways. He put his ear against the dog's stomach and said, "He says he wants cheese."
>
> This is the stuff you'll forget by next week. Unless you don't.
>
> #foreverFireflies #momlife #toddlerlife

---

### Twitter/X Posts

**Tone:** Mix of one-liner parent observations and behind-the-build founder moments. Short, resonant, human. No threads unless the story genuinely needs it.

**Format rules:**
- One tweet, one idea. If it needs a thread, it probably needs a blog post
- No hashtags in the tweet body (they break the reading flow). One at the end if needed
- 1–2 emoji max
- Don't sell the app directly in most tweets. Build affinity first. Mention the app naturally, not as a pitch
- Quote-tweet real moments (with permission) over manufactured content

**Example 1 — Parent observation:**
> Your kid said something today you'll forget by Friday. That's not a failure — that's just how memory works. We built an app because we kept losing the good ones.

**Example 2 — Build-in-public:**
> Spent the morning on push notification copy. The difference between "Don't forget to record today" and "What made her smile?" is everything. One is a guilt trip. The other is an invitation.

### In-App Copy

**Tone:** Defers to the Tone & Language table in [`docs/design/design-style.md`](docs/design/design-style.md) — that is the source of truth for all in-app writing.

**Core principles (summarized from design-style.md):**
- Warm, encouraging language — never clinical, instructive, or guilt-inducing
- Use the child's name whenever possible
- Frame features as emotional outcomes ("kept forever" not "stored in cloud")
- No streak counters, no "you missed X days"
- Encourage, don't instruct ("Or write instead" not "Switch to text input mode")
- The notification is a "gentle nudge" — never a "reminder" or "alert"

**Example 1 — Empty state:**
> No memories yet. Tap the mic and start talking — 60 seconds is all it takes.

**Example 2 — Memory saved confirmation:**
> Your voice and your words — kept forever.

## 7. Reference Copy

These 10 lines represent the Forever Fireflies voice at its best. When writing new copy and unsure of the tone, write toward these.

1. **"60 seconds. One memory. Kept forever."**
   *— Tagline energy. Three beats. No wasted words.*

2. **"She said it once. Now it's yours to keep."**
   *— The emotional core of the product in two sentences.*

3. **"You'll never forget the little things."**
   *— The brand promise. Simple, direct, true.*

4. **"What made Luca laugh today?"**
   *— Push notification. Uses the child's name. An invitation, not a demand.*

5. **"He told me the dog was his best friend. I hit record."**
   *— A real moment. No commentary needed. The copy stays out of the way.*

6. **"These days go fast. Your words don't have to."**
   *— Acknowledges the feeling without guilting. Offers the product as a quiet solution.*

7. **"Before today slips away — what's one thing worth keeping?"**
   *— Gentle urgency. Not "don't miss out" — just a soft question.*

8. **"A journal you speak into. A memory you'll read forever."**
   *— Explains the product without feature language.*

9. **"She's 3 years, 2 months old. These days go fast."**
   *— Notification copy that uses real data (the child's age) to create an emotional beat.*

10. **"Not every moment is a milestone. The ordinary ones matter too."**
    *— Positioning against the "first steps / first words" apps. We capture the in-between.*
